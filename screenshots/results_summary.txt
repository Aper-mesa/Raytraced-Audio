KEY FINDING ("LSD Blindspot"):
  - Min-Phase has LOWEST  LSD  = 0.030 dB  (LSD says "best")
  - Min-Phase has HIGHEST CPM  = 14.094    (CPM says "worst")
  - Driver: Group Delay Error  = 2.335 ms  (10x larger than any other condition)

This confirms the Andreopoulou & Katz (2022) hypothesis: magnitude-only metrics
are fundamentally inadequate for HRTF quality prediction.

Figure Descriptions:

fig4_1_metric_comparison.png
  Grouped bar chart showing normalized metric values across all 5 degradation
  conditions. The "LSD blindspot" is annotated: min-phase has near-zero LSD
  but the highest group delay error and CPM.

fig4_2_lsd_vs_cpm.png
  Scatter plot of LSD vs CPM for each condition. Min-phase appears in the
  lower-left by LSD but upper region by CPM, demonstrating the fundamental
  divergence between magnitude-only and composite perceptual metrics.

fig4_3_spectral_comparison.png
  Spectral comparison of original vs min-phase HRTF (Subject 1, left ear).
  Top: magnitude spectra nearly identical (explaining near-zero LSD).
  Bottom: magnitude difference is minimal, confirming LSD cannot detect
  the phase distortion introduced by min-phase reconstruction.

fig4_4_group_delay.png
  Group delay comparison of original vs min-phase HRTF. The massive
  divergence between the curves (especially 4-12 kHz pinna notch region)
  reveals the phase distortion that LSD is blind to.
